There is a specific kind of tired that does not respond to sleep. You lie down and your brain keeps running the same three thoughts in a loop anyway. This is the tired these mandala coloring pages were made for.

Coloring is not childish, despite what anyone implies. It occupies just enough of your attention that the looping thoughts finally have somewhere else to go. Your hands get busy. Your mind, slowly and without asking permission, gets a little quieter.

## Why Mandalas Specifically

Mandalas work better than most coloring patterns for this particular kind of evening. The repeating symmetry gives your brain a predictable rhythm to follow, which is exactly what an overactive mind needs. No new decision every three seconds, just the next petal, the next ring, the next small shape.

There is also something genuinely calming about symmetry itself. Watching a pattern complete itself in matching color, one section mirroring the last, scratches an itch most of us did not know we had.

## How to Get the Most Out of It

Print on slightly heavier paper if you have it. Standard printer paper works fine too, but cardstock holds up better against markers without bleeding through.

Pick whatever you actually have on hand. Colored pencils, markers, even a gel pen set. The tool matters far less than just sitting down and starting.

Skip the pressure to finish in one sitting. These pages are meant to be picked up and set down across several nights if that is what your week allows. There is no deadline on a coloring page, and that might be the most relaxing part of the whole thing.
